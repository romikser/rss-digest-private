🗒️ Logistics & Notes

  • Take That / Aviva Stadium (Sat): No public parking available. Best options are DART to Lansdowne Road station (right beside stadium), buses 7, 8, 45, or 84 from city centre, or Luas Red Line to Connolly + walk. Road closures enforced by An Garda Síochána approximately 2 hours before showtime. TFI event transport info →
  • Longitude / Marlay Park (Sat–Sun): No convenient Luas stop — take Luas Green Line to Balally or Dundrum and walk ~15 min. Limited car parking on site; buses 116 and 16 also serve the area. Wellies or waterproof boots essential — grass arena gets very muddy. No re-entry policy.
  • Iveagh Gardens (Sun): Small, intimate venue — sells out fast. No re-entry after entry. Bring a blanket or low folding chair. Nearest Luas: Harcourt (Green Line). No parking nearby; walk from city centre or cycle.
  • dlr Streets & Coastival (free, both days): DART to Dún Laoghaire station, a 5-minute walk to the pier. No booking needed. Runs 12pm–4pm on both days. Seafront parking in Dún Laoghaire can be tight on event weekends.
  • Weather heads-up: Saturday is the wetter day — layer up and pack a waterproof regardless of which event you're at. Sunday afternoon is forecast to brighten with sunny intervals, making it better for outdoor/seafront activities.
  • Busy weekend overall: Three major music events (Take That, Longitude, Iveagh Gardens) plus dlr Summerfest all run simultaneously — expect city centre roads and public transport to be busier than usual, especially Saturday evening around Ballsbridge and the Harcourt/Rathmines corridor.
  • Book ahead: Iveagh Gardens tickets sell out quickly. Longitude and Take That are ticketed events — check Ticketmaster.ie for availability.
