🎶 Events to Know About

  • Longitude Festival 2026
    📍 Marlay Park, Rathfarnham  ·  Sat 4 & Sun 5 July
    Dublin's biggest outdoor music festival is back over the weekend — international hip-hop, R&B, electronic and pop acts across multiple stages. Two-day tickets from ~€200.
  • Take That — Live at Aviva Stadium
    📍 Aviva Stadium, Ballsbridge  ·  Sat 4 July
    The British pop legends bring their tour to Dublin's Aviva Stadium on Saturday night. Big stadium singalong energy — expect a packed Lansdowne Road.
  • Live at the Iveagh Gardens
    📍 Iveagh Gardens, Dublin 2  ·  Sat 4 & Sun 5 July (6 PM each evening)
    The beloved open-air concert series in the walled gardens. The Scratch on Saturday, Elvis Costello & The Imposters with Charlie Sexton on Sunday.
  • Mumford & Sons + The War on Drugs
    📍 Marlay Park, Rathfarnham  ·  Sun 5 July
    A brilliant double-bill at Marlay Park on Sunday — folk-rock anthems from Mumford & Sons alongside indie rock heavyweights The War on Drugs.
  • dlr Streets Festival
    📍 Dún Laoghaire  ·  Sat 4 & Sun 5 July
    Dún Laoghaire comes alive with live music, street performances, and a festive seaside atmosphere over the whole weekend — free to attend.

💡 Things to Do

  • Saturday — Embrace the Irish rain: museums & galleries With a grey and showery Saturday, it's a perfect excuse to duck into the National Gallery of Ireland (free entry) or the Chester Beatty Library in Dublin Castle. If you want to be out, Longitude at Marlay Park is rain-or-shine — pack a poncho.
  • Sunday — Coastal walk as it clears With sunny spells building through Sunday afternoon, head to the Dún Laoghaire promenade or the Howth cliff walk to make the most of the improving conditions. Pair with the free dlr Streets Festival while you're in Dún Laoghaire.
